# AI and Robotics After Fragmentation

## Research Question

Which artificial-intelligence and robotic systems remain useful after cloud infrastructure, chip supply, and global maintenance networks fragment?

## Summary

Artificial intelligence can survive the breakdown of globalization, but not as an immaterial force.

AI depends on processors, memory, storage, electricity, cooling, networking, software, trained personnel, sensors, actuators, and replacement parts. Large frontier systems require far more infrastructure than small local models or narrow controllers.

The setting should distinguish between training, centralized inference, local inference, embedded autonomy, and simple automation.

## AI Tiers

### Tier 1 — Frontier Training Systems

Require large data centers, advanced accelerators, high-bandwidth networks, reliable grids, cooling, and industrial semiconductor access. Likely limited to a few surviving powers.

### Tier 2 — Regional General-Purpose Systems

Run in hardened data centers and serve governments, research institutions, utilities, and large cities.

### Tier 3 — Local Models

Operate on regional servers, workstations, vehicles, factories, farms, or community networks. They may be older, compressed, specialized, or periodically synchronized.

### Tier 4 — Embedded Control

Machine vision, predictive maintenance, navigation, agricultural control, and industrial automation running on modest hardware.

## Robotics Constraints

Robots are limited less by software than by:

- batteries,
- motors and bearings,
- seals and lubricants,
- cameras and sensors,
- precision gearboxes,
- power electronics,
- tires and tracks,
- corrosion,
- and skilled maintenance.

Humanoid general-purpose robots are possible but should not displace simpler machines that perform narrow tasks more efficiently.

## Causal Chain

```text
Cloud and chip supply fragment
→ frontier training concentrates in secure blocs
→ local systems stop receiving constant upgrades
→ communities preserve older models and open software
→ hardware failures reduce available computing
→ AI is assigned to high-value tasks
→ automation becomes uneven and region-specific
```

## Worldbuilding Implications

- Local AIs become inherited civic infrastructure.
- Model versions diverge culturally and technically across regions.
- Communities maintain archives of weights, documentation, and training data.
- Old autonomous tractors, warehouse systems, and inspection drones remain useful long after consumer platforms disappear.
- A town may understand how to operate an AI without being able to reproduce its accelerator hardware.
- Skilled technicians become mediators between machine recommendations and local reality.
- AI remains common enough to be ordinary in advanced settlements but scarce enough to create sharp regional inequality.

## Outcomes to Avoid

- AI functioning indefinitely without hardware replacement.
- Every surviving robot possessing human-level general intelligence.
- Cloud systems remaining globally unified after network fragmentation.
- Treating software copies as sufficient without compatible hardware and power.
- Assuming all AI disappears because frontier data centers fail.

## Proposed Canon

AI survives in layers. Frontier capability becomes concentrated, while local models and narrow automation remain widely distributed. Robotics is most durable in agriculture, logistics, maintenance, inspection, defense, and hazardous work.
