# Master Bibliography

This file indexes the major sources used across Project Anthropocene.

Detailed notes should live in domain-specific research files. The master bibliography exists to prevent duplicate work, standardize citations, and show which sources materially influence canon.

## Citation Format

Use the following structure when practical:

```text
Author or Institution. "Title." Publication or Report Series, year. DOI, report number, or stable URL. Accessed YYYY-MM-DD.
```

For datasets, include the version or release date.

For reports, include the chapter, page, table, or figure used when relevant.

## Status Tags

- `[FOUNDATIONAL]` — central synthesis or dataset used across the project.
- `[DOMAIN]` — important within a specific research area.
- `[LEAD]` — promising source not yet fully reviewed.
- `[SUPERSEDED]` — replaced by a newer edition or stronger source.
- `[DISPUTED]` — findings are materially contested or difficult to reproduce.

## Climate

### IPCC AR6 Working Group I [FOUNDATIONAL]

- **Full citation:** Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change. *Climate Change 2021: The Physical Science Basis*. Cambridge University Press, 2021.
- **Type:** assessment
- **Institution or publisher:** IPCC
- **Publication date:** 2021
- **Geographic scope:** global and regional
- **Time horizon:** present through 2300 in selected extensions
- **Scenario or pathway:** SSP1-1.9 through SSP5-8.5
- **Key relevance:** warming ranges, extremes, sea level, uncertainty, and physical climate mechanisms.
- **Used in:** `research/climate/baseline_scenario.md`, `research/climate/sea_level_bounds.md`, `timeline/2025-2100_climate_evidence.md`
- **Limitations:** scenario outputs are conditional projections, not forecasts of political history.
- **Last checked:** 2026-07-12

### IPCC AR6 Working Group II [FOUNDATIONAL]

- **Full citation:** Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change. *Climate Change 2022: Impacts, Adaptation and Vulnerability*. Cambridge University Press, 2022.
- **Type:** assessment
- **Institution or publisher:** IPCC
- **Publication date:** 2022
- **Geographic scope:** global and regional
- **Time horizon:** 21st century with selected longer-term risks

- **Used in:** `research/agriculture/wheat_heat_risk.md`, `timeline/2025-2100_climate_evidence.md`

## Agriculture and Food Systems

### Asseng et al. 2015 [DOMAIN]

- **Full citation:** Asseng, S., et al. "Rising temperatures reduce global wheat production." *Nature Climate Change* 5, 143-147, 2015.
- **Type:** peer-reviewed article
- **Geographic scope:** global wheat production
- **Key relevance:** relationship between warming and wheat yield loss across crop models and observations.
- **Used in:** `research/agriculture/wheat_heat_risk.md`
- **Limitations:** global aggregate relationships do not establish local physiological cutoffs.
- **Last checked:** 2026-07-12

### Zhao et al. 2017 [DOMAIN]

- **Full citation:** Zhao, C., et al. "Temperature increase reduces global yields of major crops in four independent estimates." *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences* 114(35), 2017.
- **Type:** peer-reviewed article
- **Geographic scope:** global major crops
- **Key relevance:** independent estimates of temperature-related yield losses for wheat and other staples.
- **Used in:** `research/agriculture/wheat_heat_risk.md`
- **Limitations:** temperature effects interact with carbon dioxide, precipitation, adaptation, and management.
- **Last checked:** 2026-07-12

### Saini, Sedgley, and Aspinall 1983 [DOMAIN]

- **Full citation:** Saini, H. S., Sedgley, M., and Aspinall, D. "Effect of heat stress during floral development on pollen tube growth and ovary anatomy in wheat." *Australian Journal of Plant Physiology*, 1983.
- **Type:** peer-reviewed article
- **Geographic scope:** experimental wheat physiology
- **Key relevance:** reproductive-stage vulnerability to heat.
- **Used in:** `research/agriculture/wheat_heat_risk.md`
- **Limitations:** controlled experimental evidence should not be generalized directly to all cultivars or field conditions.
- **Last checked:** 2026-07-12

### Sarkar et al. 2021 [DOMAIN]

- **Full citation:** Sarkar, S., Islam, A. K. M. A., Barma, N. C. D., and Ahmed, J. U. "Tolerance mechanisms for breeding wheat against heat stress: A review." *South African Journal of Botany* 138, 2021.
- **Type:** peer-reviewed review
- **Geographic scope:** global wheat breeding and physiology
- **Key relevance:** heat-tolerance mechanisms, vulnerable stages, and breeding options.
- **Used in:** `research/agriculture/wheat_heat_risk.md`
- **Limitations:** review of heterogeneous experiments and cultivars.
- **Last checked:** 2026-07-12

## Bibliography Maintenance

- Prefer stable identifiers over temporary links.
- Note when a source has been superseded, but do not silently remove it if canon previously relied upon it.
- Record the date a web source was last checked.
- Link every source to the research notes that use it.
- Avoid placing a source in multiple sections; cross-reference it instead.
- Do not treat inclusion in this bibliography as endorsement of every claim in a source.
