# Provisional History: 2100-2150

**Status:** Provisional canon

This period is remembered as the Long Retreat: the contraction of universal systems into regional political, industrial, and ecological orders.

## 2100-2109: The End of Universal Access

The institutions of the old global order still exist, but access to their benefits is no longer universal.

Likely developments:

- international finance and shipping continue along protected routes;
- many regions lose dependable access to advanced medicine, replacement electronics, aviation, and high-value industrial goods;
- national governments formally delegate or lose authority to regions, utilities, port authorities, military districts, and corporations;
- and large-scale relocation programs become permanent settlement policies.

The distinction between developed and developing economies gives way to a more fragmented divide between connected, partially connected, and excluded territories.

## 2110-2119: Functional Succession

Successor institutions emerge before old states formally disappear.

Likely developments:

- regional grids and water systems become sovereign in practice;
- city alliances coordinate food, energy, defense, and transport;
- military engineering organizations maintain dams, railways, ports, and communications;
- industrial enclaves exchange critical goods through treaty corridors;
- and local legal systems replace national enforcement in peripheral areas.

Political legitimacy increasingly depends on whether an authority can provide water, power, food, security, and repair.

## 2120-2129: The Maintenance States

The strongest surviving societies consolidate around repair ecosystems.

Likely developments:

- technical education, standards, salvage, metrology, and remanufacturing become central state functions;
- product diversity contracts in favor of durable regional designs;
- advanced artificial intelligence remains available in protected data centers and selected local systems;
- frontier chip production survives in only a few integrated industrial powers;
- and mature-node electronics become the technological foundation of most advanced regions.

Power is measured less by inherited machinery than by the ability to reproduce essential components.

## 2130-2139: Cultural Divergence

Several generations have now lived under permanent emergency and fragmented networks.

Likely developments:

- regional identities become more important than old national identities;
- climate-receiving cities develop hybrid languages, customs, and citizenship systems;
- historical narratives of responsibility for the Fracture become politically important;
- seasonal movement, nocturnal work, rationing, and civic service are normalized in many cultures;
- and old international media becomes archival culture rather than a shared global present.

The world is no longer merely surviving the collapse. It is becoming culturally distinct from the civilization that preceded it.

## 2140-2150: Stabilization Without Restoration

Some regions enter a period of political and infrastructural stabilization.

Likely developments:

- reliable trade resumes between regional powers;
- abandoned territories become salvage zones, ecological reserves, contested frontiers, or resettlement projects;
- regional communication gateways support intermittent intercontinental exchange;
- sea-level rise and ecological transformation continue despite slower emissions;
- and new federations, city leagues, river alliances, and maintenance states formalize their borders and institutions.

No serious movement can restore the seamless global order of the early 21st century. Recovery means building durable regional systems, not recreating the past.

## Boundary Conditions

By 2150, Project Anthropocene provisionally assumes:

- global warming remains elevated and may still be increasing slowly;
- sea level continues rising;
- old national borders survive unevenly as law, memory, and identity;
- advanced technology remains present but geographically concentrated;
- regional trade and communications are recovering;
- and new civilizations are emerging from institutions originally created as emergency adaptations.

The post-apocalyptic world begins when people stop expecting the old world to return.
