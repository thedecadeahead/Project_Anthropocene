# Project Anthropocene Master Timeline

**Status:** Provisional canon index

This document summarizes the current historical framework and links to the detailed era files.

## 2025-2044 — The Late Global Age

The contemporary global order remains recognizable. Climate losses, insurance retreat, industrial rivalry, and adaptation spending rise while technology continues advancing.

Emergency governance becomes permanent. Managed retreat, strategic reserves, seasonal heat restrictions, migration policy, and protected infrastructure reshape ordinary life.

Recovery capacity falls behind the pace of shocks. Food crises, fiscal failures, migration stress, conflict, infrastructure loss, and supply-chain fragmentation break the universal systems of the First Global Age.

Authority and industry contract into regional grids, maintenance states, city alliances, strategic corridors, and protected industrial systems.

## 2150 onward — Undefined Recovery Era

Future batches must establish:

- regional successor states;
- global population and settlement patterns;
- the principal setting year;
- major trade and communication routes;
- ecological conditions;
- and the relationship between inherited technology and new invention.

## Current Historical Thesis

Project Anthropocene does not have one apocalypse date.

Its central historical claim is:

```text
Climate and ecological pressure
+ unequal adaptation
+ financial and infrastructure fragility
+ migration and political exclusion
+ strategic competition
+ repeated conflict
= declining recovery capacity
```

The decisive break occurs when institutions can no longer restore normality before the next crisis.

## Canon Limitations

The current timeline does not yet establish:

- exact wars,
- exact famine events,
- named successor states,
- final borders,
- global casualty totals,
- a precise world population after 2100,
- or the definitive setting date.

Those details require regional and domain-specific research before canonization.
