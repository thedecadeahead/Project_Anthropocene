# Contributing to Project Anthropocene

Thank you for contributing to Project Anthropocene.

Contributors are called **Researchers** because the project is built through a combination of evidence, analysis, and imagination.

## Before You Contribute

Please read:

- `README.md`
- `docs/00_Project_Philosophy.md`
- `docs/01_Canon_Policy.md`
- `docs/02_Research_Methodology.md`

These documents define the project's purpose, tone, research standards, and canon process.

## What You Can Contribute

Researchers may contribute:

- research notes,
- source summaries,
- timelines,
- regional histories,
- maps,
- climate and ecology material,
- technologies,
- institutions,
- settlements,
- cultures,
- adventures,
- game systems,
- short fiction,
- editing,
- fact-checking,
- and accessibility improvements.

## Contribution Principles

### Ground Major Claims

Claims with broad consequences should include credible supporting sources and clearly labeled assumptions.

### Separate Evidence from Invention

Distinguish what is observed, projected, inferred, speculative, and fictional canon.

### Preserve Coherence

Review existing material before introducing a new event, faction, technology, or region.

Explain any conflict with established canon.

### Respect Uncertainty

Do not present uncertain projections as guaranteed outcomes.

Ranges, competing interpretations, and unresolved questions are welcome.

### Write for Humans

Use clear language. Define specialist terms. Avoid unnecessary jargon.

The setting should remain usable by readers, writers, players, and game masters who are not experts in every field.

### Leave Story Space

Avoid defining every detail. Shared settings need room for local campaigns, stories, and future contributions.

## Recommended Workflow

1. Search the repository for related material.
2. Open or join an issue for substantial changes.
3. Gather and document relevant sources.
4. Draft the contribution with a stated canon status.
5. Explain assumptions and downstream effects.
6. Submit the work for review.
7. Revise based on research, continuity, and editorial feedback.

Small corrections and formatting improvements may not require a prior issue.

## Suggested Contribution Header

Substantial documents should begin with metadata similar to:

```markdown
---
title: Example Title
status: proposal
domain: climate
time_period: 2050-2100
region: global
researchers:
  - GitHubUsername
last_reviewed: YYYY-MM-DD
---
```

Use an appropriate status from the Canon Policy.

## Research Contributions

Research notes should answer a clear question and include:

- a concise summary,
- key findings,
- uncertainties,
- worldbuilding implications,
- proposed canon if applicable,
- and complete citations.

Every source belongs in the project's single index, [`research/bibliography.md`](research/bibliography.md), which defines the standard citation format and links each source to the notes that rely on it. Add new sources there and link a note's `## Sources` entries to the matching bibliography section rather than inventing a per-file format.

Do not copy large portions of copyrighted sources into the repository. Summarize findings and quote only brief passages when exact language is important.

## Fiction and Game Material

Creative works should state whether they are:

- canon,
- canon-compatible,
- provisional,
- or non-canon.

Stories and adventures may explore ambiguity, unreliable narration, and conflicting accounts without changing shared canon.

Rules systems should avoid silently redefining setting facts for mechanical convenience. Note deliberate abstractions.

## Style

- Use Markdown for text documents.
- Use descriptive file names.
- Prefer concise headings and short sections.
- Define acronyms on first use.
- Use inclusive, respectful language.
- Distinguish in-universe writing from out-of-universe analysis.
- Use Common Era dates unless a document intentionally uses an in-universe calendar.

## Pull Requests

A pull request should explain:

- what it adds or changes,
- why the change is useful,
- its proposed canon status,
- the research supporting it,
- uncertainties or disputed assumptions,
- and which existing files may be affected.

Large contributions should be divided into coherent reviewable batches.

## Review Criteria

Contributions are evaluated for:

- relevance to the project,
- research quality,
- causal plausibility,
- consistency with existing canon,
- clarity,
- usefulness for storytelling or play,
- and respect for the project's tone and community.

A contribution may be valuable even when it is not accepted as canon. Alternate and experimental work can remain available as non-canon material.

## Corrections and Disagreement

Challenge ideas, evidence, and assumptions rather than people.

When disagreeing:

1. Identify the specific claim.
2. Provide stronger evidence or reasoning.
3. Explain the effect on the setting.
4. Propose a practical revision.

Good-faith correction is central to the project.

## Community Standard

Project Anthropocene welcomes Researchers from different disciplines, backgrounds, and experience levels.

Expertise should be shared, not used to exclude. Curiosity, transparency, and constructive criticism are expected from everyone.
