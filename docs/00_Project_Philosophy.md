# Project Philosophy

> *The future has not been written yet.*

Project Anthropocene is an evidence-informed collaborative worldbuilding project about one plausible future of Earth after humanity fails to prevent severe climate disruption, but continues to adapt, invent, organize, and survive.

The project is designed as a shared foundation for tabletop roleplaying games, short fiction, video games, sourcebooks, maps, and other forms of storytelling.

## Purpose

Project Anthropocene exists to explore a future that is neither utopian nor nihilistic.

It asks:

- What happens when climate change is not stopped in time?
- How do technological progress and institutional decline coexist?
- What forms of civilization emerge when global systems fracture?
- What does everyday life look like generations after cascading crises?
- What remains worth preserving, rebuilding, or reinventing?

The setting is not intended to predict a single inevitable future. It presents one defensible chain of outcomes grounded in current evidence, historical precedent, and clearly identified speculation.

## Core Commitments

### Evidence Before Speculation

Major claims about climate, ecology, agriculture, demography, infrastructure, economics, and technology should begin with credible research.

Fictional extrapolation is encouraged, but the transition from evidence to invention must be visible.

### Cascades, Not a Single Apocalypse

The collapse of the old global order is not caused by one event.

It emerges from interacting pressures such as:

- extreme heat,
- crop failures,
- water scarcity,
- migration,
- pandemics,
- conflict,
- financial instability,
- infrastructure loss,
- declining state capacity,
- and fractured supply chains.

No single crisis ends civilization. Their interaction reduces resilience until regional systems fail at different times and in different ways.

### Technology Continues

Scientific and technological progress does not stop merely because political and economic systems are under stress.

Artificial intelligence, robotics, biotechnology, energy systems, materials science, medicine, communications, and automation may continue to advance before and during the collapse.

The central question is not whether advanced technology exists, but who controls it, who can maintain it, and where its supply chains remain intact.

### Uneven Futures

The world does not collapse uniformly.

Some regions are abandoned. Others become refuges, breadbaskets, industrial centers, fortress states, federations, communes, or city-states.

Some communities retain advanced infrastructure. Others lose access to technologies that were once ordinary.

Regional variation is essential to the setting.

### Humanity Adapts

People do not simply wait for civilization to end.

They migrate, cooperate, exploit, repair, improvise, ration, organize, fight, preserve knowledge, form new institutions, and create new cultures.

The setting should reflect both human cruelty and human resilience.

### Hope Survives

Project Anthropocene is post-apocalyptic, but it is not a celebration of despair.

People still raise children, make art, fall in love, observe rituals, play games, argue about politics, build communities, and imagine better futures.

The old world may be gone. Human meaning is not.

## Creative Boundaries

Project Anthropocene defaults to the following boundaries:

- No supernatural forces are required to explain the world.
- No aliens are required to trigger or resolve the collapse.
- No miracle technology solves climate change without meaningful constraints.
- No society is culturally or technologically uniform.
- No major historical event should exist solely because the plot requires it.
- No faction should be reduced to a simple moral caricature.

These boundaries may be challenged only when doing so strengthens the project's central purpose and remains clearly identified as non-core material.

## Tone

The intended tone is:

- scientifically grounded,
- historically literate,
- politically complex,
- materially detailed,
- emotionally human,
- and cautiously hopeful.

The setting should feel inhabited rather than merely ruined.

## The Researcher

Contributors to Project Anthropocene are called **Researchers**.

A Researcher may be a writer, scientist, historian, artist, cartographer, game designer, engineer, editor, or enthusiast.

The title reflects the project's culture: contributors are not simply adding lore. They are helping construct a coherent future history.
