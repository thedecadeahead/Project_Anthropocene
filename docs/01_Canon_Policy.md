# Canon Policy

Project Anthropocene is a collaborative setting. A clear canon policy is necessary to keep the world coherent while still allowing experimentation.

## Canon Levels

Every substantial contribution should be assigned one of the following statuses.

### Canon

Material formally accepted into the shared setting.

Canon establishes facts that other contributors may rely upon, including:

- historical events,
- geopolitical changes,
- technologies,
- environmental conditions,
- institutions,
- settlements,
- cultures,
- and major terminology.

Canon should be internally consistent, traceable to supporting research where applicable, and reviewed before acceptance.

### Provisional Canon

Material accepted for active development but still subject to revision.

Provisional canon may be used by other contributors, but should not yet serve as the foundation for large dependent bodies of work without coordination.

### Proposal

A developed idea submitted for consideration.

Proposals should explain:

- what is being introduced or changed,
- why it belongs in the setting,
- what evidence or precedent supports it,
- what existing canon it affects,
- and what uncertainties remain.

### Research

Source material, notes, datasets, summaries, and analysis that inform the setting but are not themselves fictional canon.

Research documents should distinguish direct findings from interpretation.

### Speculation

Plausible but weakly supported extrapolation.

Speculation is welcome when evidence cannot answer a future-facing question, but it must be labeled clearly and should not be presented as settled fact.

### Non-Canon

Experiments, alternate timelines, one-shot adventures, adaptations, and creative works that use Project Anthropocene without altering the shared setting.

Non-canon work may deliberately contradict canon, provided it is labeled.

## Canon Principles

### Canon Is Additive, Not Absolute

The setting should grow through refinement rather than constant replacement.

New material should preserve existing canon when possible. Retcons should be reserved for contradictions, major research corrections, or changes that substantially improve the setting.

### Uncertainty Is Allowed

Future history should not pretend to possess impossible precision.

Some events may have:

- disputed dates,
- incomplete records,
- conflicting regional accounts,
- uncertain casualty estimates,
- or competing historical interpretations.

In-universe uncertainty can make the setting more credible.

### Scale Matters

A claim about one settlement requires less review than a claim that reshapes the climate, global population, or international order.

The broader the impact, the stronger the evidence and review standard should be.

### Canon Should Preserve Story Space

Canon should define enough to make the world coherent without explaining every mystery or deciding every regional detail.

Researchers should leave room for game masters, writers, and future contributors to create within the setting.

## Contradictions

When two contributions conflict, use the following order of resolution:

1. Prefer material supported by stronger and more relevant evidence.
2. Prefer the interpretation that requires fewer unsupported assumptions.
3. Prefer the option more consistent with established canon.
4. Preserve both accounts as an in-universe dispute when appropriate.
5. Revise existing canon only when the conflict cannot otherwise be resolved.

## Retcons

A canon revision should include:

- the material being changed,
- the reason for the change,
- the evidence supporting the revision,
- the downstream files affected,
- and a migration plan for dependent material.

Major retcons should be documented in the repository history and release notes.

## Creative Works and Canon

A story, campaign, adventure, or game is not automatically canon simply because it appears in the repository.

Creative works may be:

- canon,
- canon-compatible,
- provisional,
- or non-canon.

Canon status should be stated clearly in the work's metadata or introduction.

## Authority

Until a broader governance model is adopted, final canon decisions rest with the project maintainer.

As the Researcher community grows, this policy may be expanded to include editors, subject-area reviewers, and a formal review process.
