# Principal Setting Year: 2250 CE

**Status:** Provisional canon

Project Anthropocene's principal playable setting is **2250 CE**.

This date places the world roughly two centuries beyond the project's present-day starting point and about a century after the end of the Long Retreat.

## Why 2250

The year 2250 provides the desired balance between familiarity and transformation.

- Ruins of the 20th and 21st centuries still exist in recognizable forms.
- Some infrastructure built before the Fracture remains operational after repeated rebuilding.
- Advanced artificial intelligence, robotics, biotechnology, and energy systems survive unevenly.
- Several generations have grown up without expecting the old global order to return.
- New political cultures have had time to stabilize.
- Climate and coastlines remain in motion rather than reaching a final state.
- The old world is historical fact, not mythology, but surviving records are incomplete and politically contested.

## Generational Distance

A person born in 2250 is approximately:

- seven to nine generations removed from the early 21st century;
- five to seven generations removed from the height of the Fracture;
- and three to five generations removed from the stabilization of major successor societies.

Family stories may preserve the names of ancestors who crossed retreat zones, served in emergency governments, defended infrastructure, or founded new settlements.

## Climate Baseline

The current planning envelope for 2250 assumes:

- approximately 4.2°C global mean warming above 1850-1900 as a working central value;
- a broad uncertainty range of roughly 3.0-5.8°C;
- approximately 2.2 meters of global mean sea-level rise relative to around 2000;
- substantial local variation from subsidence, uplift, currents, erosion, and engineered defenses;
- and continued sea-level rise beyond the setting year.

These values remain provisional and should be revised as the emissions and post-2100 climate history becomes more detailed.

## Historical Perspective

People in 2250 commonly divide history into:

- the Late Global Age;
- the Permanent Emergency;
- the Fracture;
- the Long Retreat;
- and the Reconstruction generations.

These labels are not universal. Different societies teach different dates, causes, villains, and heroes.

## Playable Present

The setting year is not a dark age.

It is a period of incomplete reconnection.

Regional powers trade again. Surveyors reopen routes. Maintenance states compete for standards and technical influence. New settlements appear in former retreat zones. Old archives are recovered. Coastal maps continue to change. Political borders remain negotiable.

The central tension of 2250 is not whether humanity survived.

It is who gets to shape the civilization that comes next.

## Alternate Play Periods

The world bible may later support additional periods:

- **2045-2074:** climate emergency and political transformation;
- **2075-2114:** the Fracture;
- **2115-2150:** the Long Retreat;
- **2180-2220:** early reconstruction;
- **2250:** principal setting;
- **2300 and beyond:** later campaigns and speculative supplements.

Unless a product states otherwise, Project Anthropocene material should assume 2250 CE.
