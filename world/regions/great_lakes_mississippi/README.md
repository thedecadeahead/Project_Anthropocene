# Great Lakes–Mississippi Regional Sourcebook

**Status:** Provisional canon

The Great Lakes–Mississippi region is the first fully developed campaign area for Project Anthropocene.

By 2250, it is one of the most strategically important zones in North America because it combines:

- vast freshwater reserves,
- navigable inland waterways,
- surviving rail and industrial corridors,
- productive but climate-stressed agricultural land,
- major pre-Fracture urban ruins,
- and a dense patchwork of successor governments.

The region is not unified. It is a connected political ecosystem shaped by water control, river engineering, migration, salvage, food production, and access to dependable infrastructure.

## Regional Scope

The sourcebook initially covers:

- the Great Lakes basin,
- the upper and middle Mississippi,
- the Missouri and Ohio river systems,
- major inland ports,
- selected former metropolitan regions,
- and the corridors linking them.

## Core Themes

- Water as sovereignty.
- Repair as political legitimacy.
- Rivers as roads, borders, and weapons.
- Old cities as inhabited ruins rather than empty dungeons.
- Migration as a source of culture and conflict.
- Regional powers connected by trade but divided by law.
- Advanced technology surviving inside uneven infrastructure islands.
