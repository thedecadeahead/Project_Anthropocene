# Travel, Trade, and Communications in 2250

**Status:** Provisional canon

The world of 2250 is connected, but not continuously or universally.

Travel and communication depend on corridors, treaties, maintenance, weather, political recognition, and access to protected infrastructure.

## Travel

### Local Travel

Common methods include:

- walking and bicycles;
- electric utility vehicles;
- autonomous shuttles in stable cities;
- riverboats and ferries;
- animal transport in low-energy or difficult terrain;
- and locally maintained rail.

### Regional Travel

Regional movement often follows defended infrastructure:

- electrified rail corridors;
- navigable rivers and canals;
- maintained highways;
- coastal shipping;
- short-range aircraft;
- and scheduled convoy routes.

Travelers may need permits, sponsorship, work contracts, health records, or proof of water and food allotment.

### Long-Distance Travel

Intercontinental travel still exists but is expensive and politically controlled.

Possible modes include:

- ocean shipping;
- long-range aircraft operated by major powers;
- high-altitude airships where practical;
- military or diplomatic transport;
- and rare orbital services.

Ordinary people may never travel beyond their region, while traders, researchers, diplomats, pilots, sailors, and technical specialists move widely.

## Trade

Trade is organized around strategic necessity rather than frictionless consumer abundance.

High-value goods include:

- medicines;
- semiconductor components;
- precision tools;
- catalysts and industrial chemicals;
- seeds and biological cultures;
- bearings, motors, and power electronics;
- technical data;
- and skilled labor.

Bulk trade includes:

- grain;
- fertilizer;
- metals;
- fuel;
- timber;
- construction materials;
- and desalinated or transported water in exceptional cases.

## Trade Systems

- regional currencies;
- commodity contracts;
- labor and service obligations;
- utility credits;
- barter at local scales;
- and clearing systems between political blocs.

Universal money is uncommon, but widely trusted commodities, digital settlement systems, and neutral trade institutions provide exchange between regions.

## Communications

### Local

Local mesh networks, municipal fiber, radio, and community servers provide reliable service inside stable settlements.

### Regional

Regional intranets connect governments, utilities, schools, markets, and transportation systems.

### Long-Distance

Long-distance communication may use:

- surviving fiber corridors;
- submarine cables;
- microwave relays;
- high-frequency radio;
- satellites;
- and delay-tolerant store-and-forward networks.

Messages can cross continents quickly when gateways are functioning, then become delayed during outages, conflict, or maintenance gaps.

## Information Reliability

Information is shaped by:

- incompatible identity systems;
- propaganda;
- forged records;
- delayed verification;
- censored gateways;
- and lost archives.

Trusted couriers, technical witnesses, archivists, and neutral communications guilds are politically important.

## Campaign Implications

Travel is rarely random wilderness wandering. It is movement between islands of infrastructure.

A journey may involve:

- negotiating passage rights;
- repairing a route;
- escorting technical cargo;
- carrying data across a network gap;
- crossing climate-danger windows;
- or deciding whether an unfamiliar authority will honor old credentials.
