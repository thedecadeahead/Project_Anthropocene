# Initial Playable Regions

**Status:** Provisional regional framework

These regions are starting points for future research and sourcebooks. They are not yet fully canonized political maps.

The first playable regions were chosen because they support different campaign styles while reflecting Project Anthropocene's core themes.

## Great Lakes Basin

### Strategic Basis

- abundant freshwater relative to many other regions;
- inland shipping;
- legacy industry;
- major cities and transport corridors;
- agricultural potential under changing climate;
- and large migration pressure.

### Likely Setting Role

A contested climate-receiving region containing maintenance states, industrial city alliances, agricultural federations, old national institutions, and crowded settlement corridors.

### Campaign Themes

- water governance;
- rail and lake trade;
- industrial recovery;
- migrant citizenship;
- restoration of abandoned cities;
- and conflict between local sovereignty and basin-wide coordination.

## Mississippi-Missouri River System

### Strategic Basis

- continental freight corridor;
- agricultural and ecological importance;
- major flood and heat exposure;
- connection between the Great Lakes, interior plains, Gulf retreat zones, and central settlements.

### Likely Setting Role

A chain of river cities, mobile communities, agricultural territories, flood-control authorities, and contested logistics corridors.

### Campaign Themes

- convoy and river travel;
- levee and dam politics;
- salvage from abandoned floodplains;
- food trade;
- ecological restoration;
- and diplomacy among river authorities.

## North Atlantic Arc

Includes parts of northeastern North America, Greenland, Iceland, the British Isles, Scandinavia, and connected ports.

### Strategic Basis

- relatively strong institutions in parts of the region;
- maritime trade;
- fisheries and changing ocean ecology;
- renewable and geothermal energy;
- northern routes;
- and surviving transatlantic cables.

### Likely Setting Role

A maritime network of advanced regional powers, fortified ports, research centers, migration destinations, and contested northern resources.

### Campaign Themes

- long-distance trade;
- maritime diplomacy;
- cable and satellite maintenance;
- fisheries conflict;
- northern settlement;
- and competition among advanced maintenance states.

## Mediterranean Retreat Belt

### Strategic Basis

- severe heat and water stress;
- dense historical infrastructure;
- strategic sea routes;
- migration corridors;
- desalination-dependent cities;
- and strong regional variation.

### Likely Setting Role

A patchwork of hardened coastal enclaves, highland settlements, seasonal cities, abandoned agricultural zones, and maritime trade powers.

### Campaign Themes

- water and cooling infrastructure;
- archaeology and salvage;
- seasonal migration;
- fortress urbanism;
- smuggling;
- and disputes over defended coastlines.

## South Asian River and Highland Systems

### Strategic Basis

- enormous population history;
- heat and humidity risk;
- glacier- and monsoon-dependent water systems;
- dense cities;
- major industrial and technological capacity;
- and high regional diversity.

### Likely Setting Role

A complex network of megacity successors, river authorities, highland states, agricultural adaptation zones, and advanced industrial corridors.

### Campaign Themes

- heat-dependent urban life;
- water treaties;
- mass transit and infrastructure;
- vertical and protected agriculture;
- migration between plains and highlands;
- and competition between dense regional powers.

## East Asian Industrial Littoral and Interior

### Strategic Basis

- major inherited manufacturing capacity;
- vulnerable coastal megacities;
- strong state and regional institutions;
- semiconductor and robotics industries;
- and large internal migration potential.

### Likely Setting Role

One of the world's principal advanced-technology regions, divided between defended coastal systems, interior receiving zones, industrial blocs, and abandoned lowland districts.

### Campaign Themes

- high-technology politics;
- coastal retreat;
- autonomous infrastructure;
- industrial espionage;
- standards competition;
- and tensions between planned systems and informal communities.

## Research Priority

The first fully developed regional sourcebook should focus on the **Great Lakes and Mississippi-Missouri systems**.

This provides:

- a familiar anchor for North American players;
- direct relevance to freshwater, migration, agriculture, industry, and inland trade;
- a broad range of settlement tiers;
- and a natural campaign corridor linking cities, frontier zones, ruins, farms, and river powers.

## Canon Warning

No final political names, borders, populations, or capital cities are established here. Each region requires dedicated climate, ecology, demographic, infrastructure, and historical research before detailed canonization.
